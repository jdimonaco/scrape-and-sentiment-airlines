"slept well for a few hours"

9 reviews Graham Henrickson (Australia) 27th April 2025

✅ Trip Verified | Travelling first class on a domestic flight within America is typically not something to write home about. That is – unless you’re flying with American Airlines from LAX to JFK. If so, make sure to fly on their direct flight with an Airbus A321 aircraft that was specifically designed and configured for these transcontinental flights. In fact, it has it’s on aircraft type – an A321T (the T stands for Transcontinental). First Class is a separate cabin at the font of the aircraft. Here you’ll find 10 individual first-class open suite - 5 rows with one on either side so each has windows and direct aisle access. This was quite a surprise for a single aisle narrow body jet. There was one dedicated flight attendant for all 10 passengers so kept pretty busy for the entire flight. He was bright and attentive. The breakfast meal was a pretty average affair but my expectations were low based on the airline’s reputation. The seat converts to a full lay-flat bed and was very comfortable and with fluffy pillows and blanket. I fell asleep easily and slept well for a few hours and woke to the smell of cookies being freshly baked in the forward galley. This was a nice touch and something the flight attendant clearly enjoyed doing. The wait for the luggage to arrive at the belt was particularly long and stressful when you are getting constant phone calls from your driver to check if it’s arrived.

✅ Trip Verified | I knew there was something wrong with the entertainment system before we left LAX because I had watched the huddle of technicians from my seat. The service was practically over before it began. The courses came so fast I could barely keep up and the food was terrible.
